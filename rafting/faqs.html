Frequently Asked Questions

Is Whitewater Rafting Safe?

Statistically, there are fewer fatalities each year for whitewater rafting than swimming or even bicycling. If you're choosing a reputable rafting company like Dry Oar and following all prescribed guidelines, rafting can be a very safe and enjoyable activity.
- Joseph Harris, Safety Manager at Dry Oar

How Long Does a Run Last?

If you want a real fun-filled, action-packed day we offer the Full River Trip. We begin this trip on the upper river with approximately 1 ½ hours of rafting. We then break for about 15 minuets and start the trip on the Middle Ocoee River with approximately another 1 ½ hours on water. From check-in to check-out, expect this trip to take you around 4 ½ to 5 hours.

What if it Rains?

You are going to get wet anyway so you’ll never know the difference! In the outdoor adventure business it would be hard to do business according to the weather, so we do run our trips RAIN OR SHINE. Whitewater rafting is one of those sports where you are going to be wet from about the time you get on the river until the time you get off, but you’ll be having so much fun you probably won’t even notice. We do have splash jacket rentals available during the Spring and Fall months on cool, rainy days.
